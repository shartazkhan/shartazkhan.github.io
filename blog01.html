Human Computer Interaction - Usability

Shartaz Khan Akash

12 April, 2021

Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) is the study of how humans interact with computers and technology. It involves the design, evaluation, and improvement of user interfaces to create seamless and intuitive interactions. The focus is on understanding user needs, behaviors, and preferences to develop systems that are easy to learn, use, and navigate.

Usability, as emphasized in "The Design of Everyday Things" by Don Norman, is a critical aspect of HCI. It refers to the effectiveness, efficiency, and satisfaction with which users can accomplish tasks using a particular interface or system. By incorporating principles of usability, designers aim to enhance user experiences, minimize errors, and maximize productivity. Through iterative testing and refinement, HCI professionals strive to create technology that seamlessly integrates into the lives of users, making it more accessible and enjoyable.


Referance Book: The Design of Everyday Things by Don Norman.
